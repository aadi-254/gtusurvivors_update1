What is CGPA / SPI / CPI ?

            Many students are always confused in such type of problems. So here we try to give you a brief explanation.

CGPA: Cumulative Grade Point Average
CPI : Cumulative Performance Index
SPI : Semester Performance Index

            Here, the difference between your CGPA and SPI is that CGPA scoring is based on 5th, 6th, 7th, and 8th semester performance, whereas SPI is based on your entire 4-year performance.

In the given syllabus of each subject, there are some credits. Here in the image, there is an explanation of marks.

Percentage: (SPI/CGPA - 0.5) * 10
ex: 9.2 CGPA = 87%
